## 3. Systematic Module Breakdown

### I. Mathematical Foundations & Asymptotic Analysis
* **Asymptotic Notation:** Deep analysis of **Big-O (Upper Bound)**, **Omega (Lower Bound)**, and **Theta (Tight Bound)** as the language of performance measurement.
* **Complexity Growth:** Comparative study of growth rates ($O(1)$ to $O(2^n)$), vital for predicting system behavior as data scales.
* **Master Theorem:** A systematic approach to solving recurrence relations in recursive algorithms.


### II. Sorting & Searching Paradigms
* **Divide & Conquer:** Dissection of **Merge Sort** and **Quick Sort**, emphasizing recursive partitioning and the trade-offs between stability and auxiliary space.
* **Linear Time Sorting:** Investigation of **Counting Sort** and **Radix Sort**, providing insight into specialized hardware-friendly sorting techniques.
* **Binary Search Trees (BST):** Balancing complexity with data access, including AVL trees for self-balancing operations.

### III. Dynamic Programming (DP) & Optimization
* **Memoization vs. Tabulation:** Strategies for optimizing recursive structures by eliminating redundant subproblem calculations.
* **Classic Optimization Problems:** Detailed case studies on **0/1 Knapsack**, **Matrix Chain Multiplication**, and **Longest Common Subsequence (LCS)**.


### IV. Graph Theory & Network Optimization
* **Traversals:** Formal study of **Breadth-First Search (BFS)** and **Depth-First Search (DFS)** as the basis for pathfinding.
* **Spanning Trees & Paths:** Critical evaluation of **Kruskal’s**, **Prim’s**, and **Dijkstra’s** algorithms, comparing greedy strategies versus dynamic programming approaches.
* **Constraint Satisfaction:** Implementing **Backtracking** to prune state-space search trees, a core concept for AI heuristic search algorithms.
